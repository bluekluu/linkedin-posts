You have 40 days. Most AI teams I've talked to don't know what that means.

August 2 is when the EU AI Act becomes fully applicable for GPAI models — the transparency, disclosure, and AI-generated content marking obligations under Article 50. Non-compliance penalties: up to 7% of global annual revenue.

This is not a distant regulatory concern. It's a launch gate.

When Google was navigating the Digital Markets Act across 60+ products and 20+ teams, the failure mode was always the same: legal finished the framework in month 1. Engineering got the ticket in month 5. The deadline didn't move.

The companies that will miss August 2 already have the policies written. They just don't have the cross-functional operating model to ship them.

What that model actually needs:
— A single owner who speaks both legal and product
— Milestones tied to releases, not calendar dates
— An escalation path that doesn't go through 4 VPs

If you're a startup building AI products for EU users and you're still figuring out your Article 50 obligations, now is the time to pressure-test your readiness — not July 28.

Happy to dig into compliance-readiness architecture with anyone in the thick of this.
