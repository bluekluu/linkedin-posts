# LinkedIn Voice Guide — Kevin Luu

*Synthesized from Phase 2 research: LinkedIn best practices 2026, Alex Stamos post analysis, high-engagement T&S/AI governance post formats.*

## Core tone
Direct practitioner voice — like a senior operator talking to a peer, not a consultant selling. Specific, slightly irreverent, never preachy. First person throughout.

## Hook rules (line 1)
- Under 12 words
- Options that work: countdown number ("42 days"), contrarian claim ("The teams who fail aren't who you think"), uncomfortable truth, short declarative that escalates to a surprise
- Never: "I'm excited to share," "Thrilled to announce," or setup that requires context before the tension

## Structure pattern
1. Hook (1 line — the scroll-stopper)
2. Context/evidence (1–2 short paragraphs, 1–3 sentences each, line break between)
3. Credential anchor (1 paragraph citing the specific Meta/Google work — not "in my experience" but the actual thing: Instagram privacy program, 60-product overhaul, LLM tooling for 300 people, AR/VR hardware where no playbook existed)
4. Concrete insight — the thing they'd screenshot (bulleted lists work well; saves are 5× more powerful than likes in 2026 algorithm)
5. Closing: direct question to audience OR soft advisory CTA ("Happy to dig into this with anyone building in this space")

## Length target
200–250 words / 1,300–1,600 characters. LinkedIn truncates at ~210 characters — hook must land before "See more."

## What to avoid
- Buzzwords: leverage, synergy, unlock value, game-changing, paradigm shift
- Vague credential references: "in my experience," "I've seen this"
- Generic advice that any T&S blogger could write
- "Like and share if you agree" / "Follow me for more"

## Hashtag format
3–5 hashtags, last line, after a blank line. Mix: 1–2 broad (#TrustAndSafety, #DataPrivacy, #AIGovernance) + 1–2 niche (#KidsSafety, #GenAITrust, #EUAIAct) + optionally 1 personal.

## Algorithm signals to optimize for (2026)
- **Saves** (5× like) — posts with bulleted lists, reference-worthy stats, or clear frameworks
- **Semantic density** — specific regulation names, real product categories, real numbers
- **First-person experience markers** — algorithm now actively boosts vs. suppresses AI-generic templates
- Story-driven posts (specific situation → lesson) get 5× more comments than advice-only posts
