42 days until the EU AI Act forces chatbot disclosure.

August 2 is the Article 50 deadline. Every AI chatbot reaching EU users must disclose it's an AI — before or at the very first interaction. Non-compliance: up to 7% of global annual revenue.

Most teams I talk to have this on the compliance backlog. That's the wrong place for it.

I ran privacy compliance programs spanning 60 products, 8 global offices, and four simultaneous regulatory regimes — GDPR, COPPA, AADC, DMA. The mistake I saw most often: treating disclosure as a UI string someone adds in the last sprint.

Disclosure is an architecture decision. You need:
— A signal that knows whether AI generated the response (not a human or a template)
— A consent layer that fires before or at first interaction, not after
— Audit logging that can prove it happened at the moment it happened

The EU published its Code of Practice on AI content marking on June 10. It specifies what "clear and distinguishable" actually means in implementation. Read it before your engineering team builds their own interpretation.

If you have AI features reaching EU users and this isn't on your July roadmap, you're running out of runway.

What part of Article 50 compliance is catching teams off guard? Happy to dig into it with anyone building in this space.
